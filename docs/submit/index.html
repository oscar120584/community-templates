      What happens when you click “Open pull request”
      
    You sign in with GitHub, and the submission is published under your own account:
    
      
      
        
    • a fork of this repository is created in your account (or reused if you already have one);
      • 
        
    • its base branch is fast-forwarded to match upstream so the pull request has a clean base — if your fork has diverging commits on that branch, it is left untouched;
      • 
        
    • your files are committed to a new branch in your fork, and a pull request is opened back here from your fork.
      • 
      
    
      
    The pull request stays open in your fork until it is reviewed and merged. Nothing is changed outside of your fork.
